Maia is a licensed social worker and dedicated to supporting the emotional health and wellness of teens. She works with teenagers struggling with anxiety, depression, abuse, family conflict, and trauma. Maia is particularly focused on teaching young people how to improve their communication and coping skills. She is dedicated to continuing the important conversations surrounding mental health and well-being to reduce the stigma of mental illness and increase access to treatment.

SERVICES

Groups

Parenting Support Group

Parenting a child who struggles with depression is hard. Taking care of your needs is just as important as caring for your child.

Managing Stress & Anxiety for Teens

This group is for teens who need strategies to help manage their emotions.

  • Learn the mind-body connection to healing
  • Practice self-regulation to restore the nervous system and relax
  • Build resiliency skills and broaden awareness to relieve anxiety

Workshops

I offer customized workshops or talks on topics related to: Stress and Anxiety in Teens, Mindfulness in Schools, Parenting Teenagers, Catastrophic Thinking, Advocating for Vulnerable Children in Schools, Mental Health & Wellness. Please tell me what kind of workshop you need.

Consultations

Implementing school-wide mindfulness practices

What does well-being look like for students? What do your faculty members need? Maia offers mindfulness trainings for teachers, and consultations on successfully implementing social emotional learning (SEL) practices into your daily schedule. A child who is not emotionally stable, or who does not feel safe cannot learn. Contact me for assistance in implementing Social Emotional Learning (SEL) practices that work and to build capacity in your school community.

Mindful Practices

Do you rush through your day on autopilot, not fully aware of what you are doing? Forgetting things, rushing through lunch while multi-tasking? Does your mind spiral down into negative self-talk or prediction. If any of this sounds familiar, mindfulness practice can help you.

I was born and raised on the beautiful island of Bermuda. I earned my undergraduate degree in Psychology from Clark Atlanta University, and later my Master’s degree in Social Work from the University of Michigan. My studies were concentrated in interpersonal practice with children and families. As a graduate student, I was awarded a Public Child Welfare Fellowship where I received clinical training in the forensic interviewing of children in sexual abuse cases, cultural competence in child welfare, and courtroom skills necessary in child maltreatment cases.

Following graduate school, I worked as a family therapist for Catholic Charities in Chicago. Here I gained valuable experience providing home-based counseling to children and families in foster care. I later relocated to Atlanta where I worked as a contractor for Oasis Counseling providing home-based counseling for parents involved with the child welfare system. Both experiences helped me to understand the value of home visits for assessment and case planning in child welfare, and the importance of meeting families where they are. I later joined the Fulton County Department of Family & Children Services as a Child Protective Services Investigator where I worked on the front lines to ensure the safety and well-being of children in homes where there may be abuse or neglect.

My greatest joy has been working in my current capacity as a school social worker. School is a major source of stress for youth, and it is here that I have an opportunity to build meaningful relationships with students. I work to inspire and support students individually, in groups, workshops, and through mindfulness practices. I truly believe that a healthy mind can play a part not only in quality of life improvements but also in a person’s prognosis. In 2013, I started the first school-based mental health program in Fulton County Schools. The partnership between Tri-Cities High School and Odyssey Family Counseling allowed us to address student mental health needs in a comprehensive way to support positive behavioral changes. Eliminating student barriers to access mental health services was a critical need that I worked to address to improve outcomes for children in school.

In 2015, after a trip to Bali, I developed an interest in meditation and mindfulness. With its beautiful views, tranquil surroundings, and rich spiritual heritage, I fell in love with the island. The Balinese people have a gentle and kind way of being. I remember how peaceful I felt on the trip. When I returned home, there was a busyness of life that was distinctly different. It did not afford the opportunity to be still and quiet the mind. Over the years, I have learned that this busyness forces our brains into a different way of behaving. Practicing mindfulness has been transformative in my personal and professional life….
