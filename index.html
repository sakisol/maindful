Maia is a licensed social worker and dedicated to supporting the emotional health and wellness of teens. She works with teenagers struggling with anxiety, depression, abuse, family conflict, and trauma. Maia is particularly focused on teaching young people how to improve their communication and coping skills. She is dedicated to continuing the important conversations surrounding mental health and well-being to reduce the stigma of mental illness and increase access to treatment.

I was born and raised on the beautiful island of Bermuda. I earned my undergraduate degree in Psychology from Clark Atlanta University, and later my Master’s degree in Social Work from the University of Michigan. My studies were concentrated in interpersonal practice with children and families. As a graduate student, I was awarded a Public Child Welfare Fellowship where I received clinical training in the forensic interviewing of children in sexual abuse cases, cultural competence in child welfare, and courtroom skills necessary in child maltreatment cases.

Following graduate school, I worked as a family therapist for Catholic Charities in Chicago. Here I gained valuable experience providing home-based counseling to children and families in foster care. I later relocated to Atlanta where I worked as a contractor for Oasis Counseling providing home-based counseling for parents involved with the child welfare system. Both experiences helped me to understand the value of home visits for assessment and case planning in child welfare, and the importance of meeting families where they are. I later joined the Fulton County Department of Family & Children Services as a Child Protective Services Investigator where I worked on the front lines to ensure the safety and well-being of children in homes where there may be abuse or neglect.
